OUR HISTORY

Think Globally, Act Locally

The Industrial Revolution: The Industrial Revolution, which began in the 18th century in Great Britain, marked a significant turning point in human history. The widespread use of steam power and other new technologies led to increased production, economic growth, and improved standards of living. However, the Industrial Revolution also resulted in widespread environmental pollution, deforestation, and increased greenhouse gas emissions. These negative effects have had lasting impacts on the environment and are still being felt today

The Environmental Movement: The Environmental Movement emerged in the mid-20th century as a response to the growing awareness of the negative impacts of human activity on the environment. Key events in the history of the movement include the publication of Rachel Carson's book "Silent Spring" in 1962, which highlighted the dangers of pesticides, and the first Earth Day in 1970, which brought attention to issues such as pollution, deforestation, and endangered species. The movement has led to the development of new laws and regulations, the creation of national parks and wildlife reserves, and the promotion of sustainable practices.

The Paris Agreement: The Paris Agreement is an international treaty on climate change that was signed in 2015 by 196 countries. The agreement aims to limit global warming to well below 2 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels and pursue efforts to limit the increase to 1.5 degrees Celsius. The agreement also includes provisions for countries to regularly report their emissions and progress towards their climate goals. The Paris Agreement represents a significant step forward in global efforts to address climate change, although challenges remain in implementing the agreement and achieving its goals.
